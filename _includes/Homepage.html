Vaskar Nepal, Ph.D.

I am a fish ecologist, with interests in fish life-history, ecophysiology and quantitative modeling. My research spans small and large water bodies in freshwater, estuarine and marine systems. I particularly enjoy working at individual and population levels. I am passionate about increasing the diversity of scientific community in the natural sciences, and about increasing undergraduate participation in research. I am also constantly trying to learn more about being a better scientist, mentor, educator and collaborator.

Currently, I am a post-doctoral research associate at Virginia Institute of Marine Science. For this position, I am conducting research in three main areas:

  1. Effects of temperature and salinity on metabolic rate and consumption rate of invasive blue catfish.
  2. Spatiotemporal changes in recruitment characteristics of striped bass in tidal rivers of Chesapeake Bay.
  3. Effects of protected areas on taxonomic and functional diversity of fishes in Nepal.
In this website, I will provide updates on my research, teaching philosophy and random data analysis scripts. The scripts are just as much for my future self, as for others. Short Bio

Vaskar is native to, as you may have guessed, Nepal, where he completed his B.Sc. in Environmental Science. He subsequently received his B.S. and MS in biological sciences from Eastern Illinois University. Vaskar completed his Ph.D. in 2020, studying the invasion ecology of blue catfish with Dr. Mary Fabrizio at Virginia Institute of Marine Science (VIMS), William & Mary. Following his Ph.D., he remained at VIMS for his post-doctoral fellowship with Drs. Mary Fabrizio and Troy Tuckey. Vaskar's research focuses on combining life-history information from the wild with laboratory experiments and quantitative techniques to inform management and conservation of aquatic fauna.
