Vaskar Nepal, Ph.D.

I am a fisheries ecologist, with interests in fish life-history, ecophysiology and quantitative modeling. Currently, I am a post-doctoral research associate at Virginia Institute of Marine Science. For this position, I am conducting research in three main areas:

  1. Effects of temperature and salinity on metabolic rate and consumption rate of invasive blue catfish.
  2. Spatiotemporal changes in recruitment characteristics of striped bass in tidal rivers of Chesapeake Bay.
  3. Effects of protected areas on taxonomic and functional diversity of fishes in Nepal.
